Galaxy-galaxy strong lensing (GGSL) tension and the deep connection with BCG masses

Meneghetti et al. (2020) showed a tension between ΛCDM simulations and GGSL observations. Unfortunately, AGN feedback do not improve the issue (contrary to what was hinted by other works). I found that this tension is deeply connected with the fact that most hydrodynamic simulations when trying to fit the SMF ends up over-estimating BCG masses. Read more: Ragagin et al. (2022a)

What characterise X-ray faint galaxy clusters?


X-ray observations could be biased towards peaked and centrally peaked gas distribution. It is possible that they miss a tail of X-ray faint objects. In Ragagin et al. (2022b) In that work I showed that they are gas-poor because they old and relaxed so the cluster had time to get depleted from the gas.

Self-interacting dark matter (SIDM) in galaxy clusters

This promising particle-physics motivated dark matter model can strongly influence the evolution of haloes. In Ragagnin et al. (2024) I present on of the first simulation suites (based on Dianoga ICs, see Rasia et al. 2014) of galaxy clusters with full baryon physics and SIDM. We found that SIDM can significantly alter the satellite abundancy.

A modular, GPU-friendly code for n-body simulations: hotwheels

I am working on a flexible and modular new implementation of a Gadget-like code (temporary project name, hotwheels), incorporating more than a decade of lessons I learned working with HPC. Designed to exploit multicore and GPU architectures, focused on modularity and miniapps (necessary to cooperate with PC facilities and GPU vendors). Follow the progress on mastodon: astrodon.social/@HotWheelsSims.

Mock multi-wave length observations for Euclid

I lead a first-author paper for the Euclid Collaboration: Ragagnin et al. (2025) . In this large-collaboration work produced mock observations from various possible multi-wave length observations (X-ray, SZ, optical and inferred weak lensing signal). In present the first covariance matrix for realistic mock-observations between all these observations, by also showing how projection effects impacts it.

g3read library

The github.com/aragnin/g3read repository contains various python tools to read, post process Gadget2/3 snapshots (expecially Magneticum outputs with the INFO block) and FoF/subfind catalgoes efficently

hydro_mc library

The package hydro_mc implements Ragagnin et al. (2020) mass, concentration, and sparsity parameters conversions between virial, and Δ = 2500, 500, 200 overdensities (both mean and critical). See the hydro_mc webapp and repository github.com/aragagnin/hydro_mc.

GPU porting of gravity and hydrodynamics

I made the effort of bringing for the first time all full-physics modules: gravity, SPH density, SPH hydrodynamics and thermal conduction) on GPUs. This was a large collaborative effort that involved several hackathons and long-term collaborations with CSCS, PGI, and nVIDIA (see my CV). See my paper: Ragagnin et al. (2020)
